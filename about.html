Understanding Hemorrhoids (Piles)

Hemorrhoids, also known as piles, mulvyadh, or bavasir, are swollen veins located at the junction of the rectum and anus. This condition arises due to increased pressure and inflammation in the veins and tissues of the anal canal—a critical passage linking the rectum to the external anus. The anal canal contains a dense network of blood vessels, making it prone to damage under strain.

Causes & Symptoms

Common causes of hemorrhoids include:

  • Excessive pressure from heavy lifting, prolonged sitting, or chronic constipation
  • Straining during bowel movements
  • Pregnancy and obesity

If untreated, hemorrhoids may lead to:

  • Bleeding during bowel movements
  • Severe pain and itching
  • Prolapsed or thrombosed hemorrhoids (advanced stages)
